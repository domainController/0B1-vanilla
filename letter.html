Terms are outlined in the attached confidential letter.

Strategic upside is embedded and can be activated — through licensing, equity positioning, and long-term operational regeneration.

[0] Moral Indemnity Structure (MIS) — Strategic Definition

The MIS is a pre-contractual protocol designed to compensate for non-financial damage (ethical breaches, opacity, symbolic misalignment) that was absorbed, neutralized, or rendered legible by the operator.

This is not a bonus, not a retroactive premium, and not a litigation substitute. The MIS is a non-emotional, non-litigious mechanism — priced as an operational shielding cost to prevent long-tail disruption.

It is also designed to cover the operational setup of the next phase: including legal structuring, contractual framing, and the 45-day adaptation window. This includes legal advisory fees, software, travel, onboarding clarity, and partner alignment.

It follows a logic of trust restoration, reputational friction compensation, and contractual clarity. It is declared upfront, and integrated as a line item within the realignment process.

The MIS also serves as a launch fund for assembling a strategic cohort — including former VPs, underused operators, and silent experts — whose task will be to assess and define the operational architecture and monthly value tiers for the 12-month framework contract.

These figures, often dismissed or overlooked by their own systems, will help calculate the real cost of integrity, continuity, and scale. This is not retribution. It is structured repair.

This initial cohort — including two strategically underutilized former VPs and a legal advisor — will be tasked with designing the contractual framework, value tiers, and phase structure of the 12-month deployment.

Their mandate is temporary: their mission ends at contract stabilization. A separate long-term governance board — composed of a senior legal anchor, a former hospitality executive, a technology strategist, and a fiscal governance expert — is scheduled to be formally convened once alignment is secured.

The MIS also acknowledges prior damage — including unpaid work, exposure without protection, reputational risk, and months of structural stress endured under unclear roles and violated agreements. This is not forward funding. It is partial compensation for what was already absorbed — silently, and at cost.
