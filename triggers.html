Trigger Events

When a professional conflict remains unresolved, the system initiates a structured escalation process. It begins with conflict parameters — explicitly or implicitly captured — that trigger a Resolution Instance. This instance activates predefined clauses and trigger layers, depending on observed behavior: refusal, delay, silence, misalignment. From there, a Contract of Resolution may be requested, leading to either a negotiated outcome or a predetermined series of actions. The process ends with a strategic closure — through formal settlement, autonomous exit, or ecosystem leverage.

Trigger Classification

1. Condition-Based Triggers

  • Inaction beyond deadline → Launch of resolution sequence
  • Failure to reply → Escalation to external visibility
  • Refusal to acknowledge → Conversion to irreversible disclosure

2. Clause-Based Triggers

  • Breach of a signed clause → License retraction + artifact dissemination
  • Delay in clause compliance → Public indexing of framework artifacts
  • Violation of contact protocol → Triggering of Sovereign Contact Layer™

3. Signal-Based Triggers

  • Detection of structural misalignment → Deployment of framework-based analysis
  • Third-party alert from trusted signal → Indirect activation of scrutiny layer
  • Trace of narrative hijack → Propagation of protective counter-structure
