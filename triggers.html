Trigger Events

Trigger Protocols™ – Definition Draft (V0)

“Every clause becomes inert without its trigger. Every trigger is void without a structure to receive it.”

When a professional conflict remains unresolved, the system initiates a structured escalation process. It begins with conflict arguments — explicitly or implicitly captured — that trigger a Resolution Instance. This instance activates predefined clauses and trigger layers, depending on observed behavior: refusal, delay, silence, misalignment. From there, a Contract of Resolution may be requested, leading to either a negotiated outcome or a predetermined series of actions. The process ends with a strategic closure — through formal settlement, autonomous exit, or ecosystem leverage.

Trigger Classification

1. Condition-Based Triggers

  • Inaction beyond deadline → Launch of resolution sequence
  • Failure to reply → Escalation to external visibility
  • Refusal to acknowledge → Conversion to irreversible disclosure

2. Clause-Based Triggers

  • Breach of a signed clause → License retraction + artifact dissemination
  • Delay in clause compliance → Public indexing of framework artifacts
  • Violation of contact protocol → Triggering of Sovereign Contact Layer™

3. Signal-Based Triggers

  • Detection of structural misalignment → Deployment of framework-based analysis
  • Third-party alert from trusted signal → Indirect activation of scrutiny layer
  • Trace of narrative hijack → Propagation of protective counter-structure

Trigger Clauses – Irreversible Activation Points

These clauses require no external approval and activate automatically when their conditions are met. They belong to the No-Capture Zone™ and enforce structural consequences without recourse.

  • 45-Day Clause
    Trigger: no payment or formal response within 45 days.
    Effect: public release of artefacts (Git, multi-level dissemination, press outreach), narrative lock.
    Recovery: not possible.
  • GP / LP Co-signature Clause
    Trigger: absence of a strategic sponsor or guarantor within the board or shareholder structure.
    Effect: automatic disqualification of the deal, framework freeze, suspension of ethical commitments.
    Recovery: only possible through public repositioning by a GP or LP.
  • Behavioral Traceability Clause
    Trigger: any attempt to interact informally or contact outside the formal protocol.
    Effect: activation of the Sovereign Contact Layer™, interaction logging, external posture lock.
    Recovery: not applicable – such behavior is considered a definitive signal.

Each clause functions as an ethical auto-executing switch, ensuring the sovereignty of the framework regardless of silence or avoidance strategies.
