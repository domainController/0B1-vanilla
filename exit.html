2.2 Symbolic Accorporation Audit — Spin-Off Ventures

🍔 Spin-Off Ventures

📍 Steakhouse Origins – Deferred Trust, Controlled Capital

📅 Between 2000 and 2014, Saahil Chudasama appears only as suppleant (alternate) in company protocols of Steakhouse Lilla Torg AB. Despite describing himself as “CEO & Part Owner”, official records show he had no real authority or control during this period.

🧾 In 2010, at age 30, he holds just 45 shares out of 1,500, equal to 3%. His father Jayantilal Chudasama holds 94%, and all voting power. No role in decision-making. His name is absent from license applications and board signatories.

⚠️ A key record from 2012 — alcohol license transfer — confirms the governance perimeter: the board is limited to his father and his brother-in-law Joachim Hedén. Saahil remains alternate. At age 32, he is still considered too peripheral for operational trust.

🧠 The pattern suggests symbolic succession without functional trust. Even after his father's death in 2014, Saahil becomes only “Managing Director” — while his mother Ingrid Jeanette Chudasama remains Chair and majority shareholder (75–80%). He holds at most 3–5% of shares.

🚧 Sliders & Shakes – Franchise as Financial Escape

📅 In 2017, Saahil (37) launches Sliders & Shakes AB. Unlike the family restaurant, this new company is located in Malmö Central Station — high visibility, small surface, optimized for scalability. It is clearly structured as a franchise pilot.

👨‍🍳 Press interviews and early team composition — including Sachin Gore, reportedly from Five Guys — confirm the format: miniature gourmet burgers, U.S. branding, handcrafted shakes. The ambition is not stability, but scale, visibility, and capital leverage.

📉 The venture fails. Company records later reveal bankruptcy, closing the expansion route. But the intent remains readable: Sliders & Shakes was a bid for independence. It was about escaping the slow, invisible stagnation of his family structure.

🧬 In founder psychology, such moves resemble “narrative resets” — where the founder rebrands himself through a new vehicle. This one was engineered for replication and capital, not craft or legacy.

📈 Quan Restaurant – Second Attempt, New Surface

📅 In 2018, Saahil launches a second company: Quan Restaurant. This time in partnership, but still within walking distance from the family restaurant. It positions itself as premium pan-Asian, nightlife-adjacent.

🧩 While not structurally a franchise, Quan reflects the same DNA: external partnerships, independent capital, upscale branding. It is not an artisanal detour — it's a continuation of the escape model, this time with risk-sharing.

📌 From Steakhouse to Quan, the pattern holds: fractional ownership, deferred recognition, repeated detachment. Not rebellion, but redirection — always away from the family nucleus, always toward capital control.

📊 Revenue Correlation – Partnership Revenue

Between 2022 and 2023, Elite Rosen Malmö AB consistently reported 3.7M SEK in “Other operating income” (Övriga rörelseintäkter). During this exact period, HSSC Event AB’s profits surged from 2.2M to 5.3M SEK, marking their most profitable cycle.

The timing and stability of these entries raise the likelihood that these 3.7M SEK represent fixed fees linked to the restaurant partnership — potentially structured as a blend of base rent and service commissions. In conventional hotel-restaurant arrangements, such income is classified under Other Operating Income rather than direct rent, especially when tied to performance-based agreements or profit sharing clauses.

The repeated value across both years suggests a fixed contractual structure rather than variable revenue participation. This supports the interpretation of a guaranteed stream of 3.7M SEK annually, likely covering both commercial access and hospitality brand use.

Such a configuration would ensure Elite’s passive return on assets without direct involvement in food & beverage operations, while granting HSSC Event AB full operational freedom and revenue retention beyond the fixed payout.

This financial logic aligns perfectly with the dual structure described in Holding Strategy, where ownership is split between operating and holding entities to facilitate cash flow extraction and risk insulation.

  • Respect des figures, mais clarté sur les rôles

5. ExitHub — Pilotage post-Exit, responsabilités croisées

  • Centralise la gestion narrative et tactique de sortie (Henrik / Saahil).
  • Outil de pilotage pour board : évite panic move, crée un sas.
  • Permet un exit lisible sans désaveu public.

🔁 Onboarding Procedures

  • All recruits (e.g., dishwasher, cook, bartender) receive an onboarding doc:
    • Submit personnummer and bank details
    • When & how salary is paid
    • Contact points by task (manager, accountant, shift lead)
    • Group messaging link (Slack or WhatsApp)
    • Emergency protocols, absence policy, late arrivals

🔚 Offboarding Checklist

  • Return of keys and materials
  • Final payment verification with breakdown
  • Simple exit feedback (suggestion form)
  • Record of extra contributions to tasks (optional)

🗂️ Role Definitions & Documentation

  • Every role has a clear task sheet and accountability map
  • Clickable job labels: e.g. Bartender, Dishwasher, Manager
  • Each includes:
    • Daily routine (opening/closing/checklist)
    • Photo or video proof (linked from phone)
    • Extras completed: cleaning ovens, late stays, setup help
    • Updates logged monthly and versioned (v1.0, v1.1...)

🏗️ Knowledge Base & Process Library

  • Centralized document hub with:
    • SOPs per role (Standard Operating Procedures)
    • Problem log + resolutions
    • Examples: Swedish for Waiters, Shift Recovery Cheat Sheet
  • Version tracking via GitHub or shared doc history
  • Visible contributor score for internal participation

📣 Communication & Troubleshooting

  • Slack group with structured channels:
    • #urgent, #rotation, #supplies, #culture, #problem-reporting
  • Every shift covered by on-duty responder (posted)
  • Visible communication tree in kitchen + printed docs

🛠️ Tool Support & Software Tutorials

  • Each tool or system (Kassa, Menu, Booking, Card Reader...) gets:
    • Usage guide with screenshots/videos
    • Contact for first-level troubleshooting
  • Slack channel dedicated to tool questions
