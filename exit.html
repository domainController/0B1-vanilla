🧭 Methodology & Disciplines Used in This Chronology

This investigation relies on a multidisciplinary approach combining law, governance, financial tracing, and structural genealogy. It draws from investigative methods used in legal forensics, compliance auditing, and influence mapping.

  • Corporate Genealogy: Tracks ownership succession, entity transitions, and long-term structural transfers (e.g., control, naming, rights).
  • Forensic History: Reconstructs undocumented events or turning points using official traces and first-hand narratives.
  • Shadow Governance: Reveals who holds practical power or makes key decisions without being formally listed on legal records.
  • Forensic Accounting: Analyzes capital flows, internal loans, and suspicious asset shifts—often used in compliance or cross-ownership audits.
  • POWER(E) Method: A diagnostic tool that assesses control through 5+1 angles — Properties, Operations, Wallets, Employees, Reputation.
  • Liability Mapping: Helps determine who actually holds legal and reputational risk in hybrid or shared-governance companies.

📌 These frameworks allow us to map who acts, who controls, who profits, and who represents — even when paper trails suggest otherwise. Especially relevant in cases involving family businesses, founder-linked ventures, or parallel ownership networks.

🔍 Trigger & Diagnosis

Trigger event: The governance crisis erupted after an altercation between Sahar and an employee, under Henrik Siebert's passive watch. The incident revealed the absence of strategic guardrails and a deeply informal management structure.

Diagnosis

Henrik failed by inertia, Sahar by aggression. No visible communication between the two leads. Responsibilities were avoided on both sides. Parallel tracks, weak stress management. The collapse stemmed from a lack of initial and strategic alignment.

Design Flaw

Putting two people at the top of a new venture means forming a co-founding team. Investors never fund ideas — they fund teams. A real team means shared values, shared history, and the ability to handle tension without breaking.

The Miscast

The actual design flaw wasn’t Sahar’s behavior — it was Sahail’s absence from operations. He had the history, the adversity, the shared scars with Henrik. He should have been in the room.

Instead, Sahar — a first-time operator with poor stress resilience — was left to absorb daily tension she wasn’t trained for. The core failure wasn’t intent, but a misplaced delegation of roles.

Founder's Job Misunderstood

One of the founder’s role is to handle the hardest problems. Same with tensions. It’s your job to tackle uncomfortable issues and initiate difficult conversations. If you avoid those, you’re not doing your part.

What Could Have Saved It

In a proven business model like restaurants, you can offset weak chemistry — but only if you write everything down. Mission, vision, values, roles, culture — all must be explicit. Then you talk. You sit down before, and you keep talking throughout.

📊 Final Note

This wasn’t a failure of intention. It was a failure of design. The structure wasn’t built for tension. And when pressure came, it broke where no foundation had ever been laid.

🧭 Henrik Siebert – Strategic Exit Guidance

After five years of leadership, Henrik has turned the site into a benchmark for high-end dining in Malmö. Revenue reached 41.1 MSEK in 2023 with an after-tax profit of 5.29 MSEK. The restaurant holds a three-week waiting list and has become a cash engine and reputational pillar for the hotel group.

But Henrik's late-stage posture reveals a growing value misalignment with the group. His role shifted toward financial extraction over long-term strategic alignment. This logic also shaped the operational structure — including incoherent role assignments and unstable management pairings.

We recommend a clean, honorable exit, built on:

  • ✅ Recognition: Acknowledge Henrik as the founder and architect of the premium model.
  • 📤 Transition: Plan a short-term disengagement with clear handovers and limited visibility.
  • 💸 Buyout: Propose a controlled financial settlement to avoid long-tail disruption or claims.

This ensures: no public scandal, no internal fracture, and a narrative of strategic maturity, not expulsion. It preserves investor confidence and reinforces the hotel’s premium integrity.
